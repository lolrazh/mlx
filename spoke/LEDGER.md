## How to Read This

- **Clean accuracy** = generic or v2 prompt only (no few-shot, no data leakage). The only honest metric.
- **Spoke accuracy** is crossed out where shown — 4/23 test examples leaked into the few-shot prompt. See [Data Leakage](#data-leakage) below.
- **Data v1** = 472 train, generic v1 system prompt (~30 tokens), includes multi-command category.
- **Data v2** = 447 train / 20 valid / 23 test, v2 system prompt (~80 tokens), multi-command removed, targeted self-correction + quote-endquote fixes, XML tag fixes.
- **Accuracy** = (exact + semantic) / N. Test set: 12 examples (v1) or 23 examples (v2).
- **DWQ 4-bit was the deploy quant** (96%, 2.1 GB) but weights were lost in cleanup. T3-v5 MLX bf16 is the only surviving model.
- **Broad eval** = 58 unseen examples (`test_set_evals.json`), 0 overlap with training. Best: T4-v5split ckpt2000 at 74%.
- Benchmark results as JSON: `spoke/bench/result_*.json`

### Learned quantization (DWQ)

DWQ = Distilled Weight Quantization. Uses bf16 model as teacher to fine-tune quantization scales/biases (125.9M trainable params, 3.1% of total). Calibrated on v4 training data (512 iters, ~42 min, 14.1 GB peak).

| Quant | Size | BPW | Accuracy | Exact | Sem | Part | Fail | Latency | Delta vs bf16 |
|-------|------|-----|----------|-------|-----|------|------|---------|---------------|
| **DWQ 4-bit** | **2.1 GB** | **4.5** | **96%** | **22** | **0** | **1** | **0** | **0.88s** | **-4%** |

DWQ 4-bit failure: emphasis #21 (drops "absolutely" — same as naive 6-bit). Training data gap, not quant artifact.

### Summary

| Method | Size | Accuracy | Latency | Notes |
|--------|------|----------|---------|-------|
| bf16 | 7.5 GB | 100% | 1.82s | Reference |
| naive 6-bit | 3.1 GB | 96% | 0.94s | Good but large |
| **DWQ 4-bit** | **2.1 GB** | **96%** | **0.88s** | **Deploy target. Same accuracy as 6-bit, 33% smaller, fastest.** |
| naive mixed 4/6 | 2.2 GB | 91% | 0.87s | Generic heuristic underperforms DWQ |
| naive 4-bit | 2.1 GB | 87% | 0.78s | DWQ recovers +9 pts at same size |

**Ad-hoc generalization tests (5 novel inputs, not in train/test):** bf16=5/5, **DWQ 4-bit=5/5**, 6-bit=3/5, mixed 4/6=2/5. DWQ matches bf16 on all ad-hoc tests. Naive quant models fail on self-correction and complex spell-replacements — test set accuracy flatters them.

**Verdict:** DWQ 4-bit (2.1 GB, 96%, 0.88s) was the deploy target. Matches naive 6-bit accuracy at 33% less size and 2x faster than bf16.

All local adapter weights and fused model weights were also lost. T3-v5 MLX bf16 (`spoke/models/spoke-qwen3-t3-v5-mlx/`) is the only surviving model. DWQ quantization of T3-v5 is pending.

114. **Shrink FLOORED at g64 (3.1 GB / 3.41 GB peak / 79.3%); structural cuts don't help; prompt-caching is the latency lever — median 488 ms clean (2026-07-13).** After g64 (2-bit-emb DWQ, finding #112) pushed further and hit walls: **`mixed_3_6` body = DEAD END** (3.1 GB / broad58 62% — 3-bit body craters copy-edit precision ~17 pts, and no size win because the 2-bit embeddings dominate the footprint); **English-safe vocab pruning** (165K vocab, 63% kept, zero-OOV validated via byte-fallback) **= NOT WORTH IT** (2.8 GB / 3.01 GB peak / broad58 72% + 1 hard fail — traded 7 pts quality for 0.4 GB peak RAM + an English-lock; byte re-decomposition of dropped tokens is the cost); **MatFormer E2B slice = IMPOSSIBLE** (Gemma 4 DROPPED MatFormer — shipped E2B is a separately-trained hidden-1536 model, not a nested slice of E4B's hidden-2560, so no free body-halving; under-2 GB needs a paid `gemma-4-E2B` Modal fine-tune). **Real RAM measured (MLX peak): baseline 4.28 → g64 3.41 GB (~20% cut); under-3 GB at full quality is unreachable by quant/vocab-prune.** **LATENCY (clean idle-machine, lossless greedy + persistent system-prompt cache): g64 = median 488 ms / mean 628 ms / p90 1207 ms, vs no-cache median ~928 ms — prompt-caching ~halves the median and IS the lever** (PLD stays rejected: unreliable, lossless on g64 but lossy on the baseline). p90 tail (long outputs) is the only miss vs the 800 ms ceiling. Prompt-caching NOT yet wired (no serving shell — TODO Track A). **Deploy pick: g64 + prompt-caching.**

113. **PLD (prompt-lookup decoding) is a BUST on Gemma 4 E4B; persistent system-prompt CACHING is the real latency lever — median ~500 ms, lossless (2026-07-13, `spoke/bench/pld_bench.py`).** For a text *cleaner* whose output echoes its input, PLD "should" win — but it doesn't here: best config (n3,k3) is only ~1.05×, and aggressive k=8 is SLOWER (0.86×). Measured cause: the 262K-vocab lm_head + gemma3n per-layer compute make multi-token verification non-free — forward cost is flat only to block-2 (block-1 47.6 ms ≈ block-2 45 ms) then rises steeply (block-4 61 ms, block-9 161 ms), so large drafts get punished on low-acceptance examples. WORSE: PLD is NOT bit-lossless on this MLX/gemma3n stack — block-forward numerics differ enough from the single-token forward to flip the argmax on near-ties (0.5-logit gap in the 262K head), silently changing output (dropped a whole clause on broad58 id53). The genuine win the sweep surfaced: the fixed 89-token v2 system prompt is ~75% of the prompt and prefill alone is ~492 ms = HALF of total latency, so persistent `make_prompt_cache()` of the system prompt (lossless — bit-identical on all 58) cuts mean 1044→~640 ms / median 928→~505 ms (~35%). **Sub-500 ms is a MEDIAN reality with caching alone**; PLD n2,k1 pushes median to ~395-496 ms but carries the near-tie-flip risk. RECOMMENDATION: adopt system-prompt caching + plain greedy (safe, ~35%); skip generic PLD, or gate n2,k1 behind an equality re-check. Floor ~250-400 ms for short inputs; p90 tail ~900-1350 ms from long outputs + M4 thermal variance. Also note: draft-model speculative decoding was pre-ruled-out (draft overhead erases the win on a 4 GB model).

112. **Dense E4B champion shrink — 2-bit embeddings + DWQ = 3.3 GB at champion-parity broad58, 0 hard fails (2026-07-13, `spoke/shrink/asym_quant.py`).** Deployed DWQ 4-bit is 3.9 GB; teardown shows ~48% of it is embedding LOOKUP tables (PLE `embed_tokens_per_layer` [262144×1344] = 39%, token embed 9%) which tolerate lower precision than the matmul body. Asymmetric quant (both embeddings → 2-bit g32, body stays 4-bit g64) + DWQ-heal (512 samples vs bf16 teacher) → **3.3 GB / broad58 78% local (42 exact) / 0 hard fails / heal val-loss 0.038**, vs the re-run 4-bit baseline 3.9 GB / 76% / 0 fail / loss 0.039. i.e. 2-bit embeddings MATCH 4-bit at 0.6 GB smaller and ~40% faster wall-clock, no garbage/thinking-runaway — **DWQ defeats the PLE-quantization-garbage problem** (the learned-scalar `ScaledEmbedding` amplifies RTN error, but teacher-guided scale/bias healing absorbs it, even on the tied output-projection `embed_tokens`). g64 variant (2-bit emb group-64) DWQ-healed is the DEPLOY PICK: **3.1 GB / broad58 79.3%**, beating g32 (3.3 GB/78%) and baseline (3.9 GB/76%) on both axes. Shrink round 2 also confirmed **`mixed_3_6` body is a DEAD END: 3.1 GB / 62.1% broad58** — 3-bit body craters quality ~17 pts for NO size win (embeddings dominate), so the body must stay 4-bit. Quantization floor reached at ~3.1 GB; vocab pruning is the only remaining <3 GB lever (but domain-locks the vocab — risky for a general dictation cleaner). CAVEAT: local grader reads 76-78 where cloud reads 82.8 — quant-vs-quant on the same local grader is valid; absolute 82.8-parity needs a cloud re-grade (TODO). NEXT lever: `mixed_3_6` body on top → ~2.5 GB (risk shifts to the capability-critical body); 3-bit embeddings are NOT a fallback (only reach ~3.5 GB). Feasibility of sub-3 GB dense deployment PROVEN.

111. **MoE expert-flashing (stream experts from SSD) is INFEASIBLE for Spoke's 2-3 GB / <800 ms target — diffuse routing kills it (2026-07-12, `ifp/stream_sim.py`).** User's idea: fine-tune Gemma 4 26B-A4B (25.2B/3.8B-active MoE) and flash inactive experts from disk for big-capacity/low-RAM (à la Apple "LLM in a Flash" + GLM-on-25GB repos). Offline streaming-cache sim over the saved decode routing traces (finding #99): because routing is DIFFUSE (one example fires ~76% of experts incl. prefill; 96.3% over broad58), a static hot-set cache needs K=64/128 experts (~50%, ~6.9 GB at 4-bit) for a 95% hit rate → sub-800 ms, but fitting 2-3 GB forces K≈16 → 61% hit → ~110 ms/decode-token + a ~3 s cold prefill of the detailed prompt = multi-second latency. **~3× over the RAM budget and ~6× over the latency budget at once — not a near-miss.** mlx-lm has no expert-offload (issue #1438) and fuses the expert bank at load anyway. Streaming's niche is running models too big to fit at all at 1-5 tok/s — the opposite of a real-time budget. The deployed 3.9 GB DENSE champion already beats every streaming operating point → shrink the dense model instead (finding #112). Also settled: `tie_word_embeddings=True` (E4B `embed_tokens` is the output projection); llama.cpp Gemma 4 PLE support now EXISTS (#22243 closed) but MLX is 20-90% faster at this size, so no reason to switch.

110. **Qwen3.5-4B HP catch-up (2026-07-09/10): 3-seed noise band = 96 core23 (flat) / 64-71 broad58; LoRA+ optimizer (same lr=1e-5 base as Gemma) closes most of the gap in one run — 100/76/88.** User asked why Gemma got a full hyperparameter research treatment (finding #107) but Qwen3.5-4B never did. Three parallel research agents found: (a) no Qwen-specific LoRA cookbook exists, but Alibaba's own framework (ms-swift) documents LoRA lr=1e-4 vs full-FT lr=1e-5 for the Qwen3 family — our recipe's lr=1e-5 matched their full-FT rate, not their LoRA rate; (b) a real ICML 2025 paper (Galim et al., "PEFT of State Space Models") shows LoRA is effective on linear projection matrices but fails on SSM/recurrent-state matrices — our DeltaNet target list (`in_proj_qkv/z/a/b`, `out_proj`) is exactly the "safe" projection class, confirming the target-module choice was architecturally correct, not lucky; (c) community coverage (Unsloth, Axolotl) is thin and inconsistent on DeltaNet module naming, but Axolotl's closest analog (Qwen3-Next) validated our own naming exactly (checked directly against the installed transformers source). A Fable second-opinion consult caught that the "single recipe, no HP search" framing was wrong (two prior HP deviations existed: rsLoRA r=16 and lr=5e-5, both regressions — finding #82/#84) and fixed the experiment design: gate on seed noise before spending on HP arms, use paired per-example comparison, and run LoRA+ at the SAME lr=1e-5 base that's proven for Gemma rather than chaining it to a possibly-noisy winning LR from another arm. **Gating (3 seeds, existing recipe, post eos-fix — see finding #109): 96% core23 flat across all 3 seeds, 64-71% broad58 (7-pt spread) — mean ~67.3, a real ~16-pt gap vs Gemma's 82.8% broad58.** **LoRA+ (optimizer=loraplus, lr=1e-5 base, r=8/α=16/dropout=0.05, single seed=42): 100% core23 / 76% broad58 / 88% v5-131** — clears the noise band on both core23 and broad58 (not a lucky seed at the edge of the band), ties Gemma on v5-131 (88 vs 88.5), still beats Gemma on core23 (100 vs 95.7), remains ~7 pts behind on broad58 (about the same size as the noise band itself). User declined further per-arm seed-confirmation (cost-conscious call — single-seed result accepted as the recipe going forward) and requested one combined arm instead of testing A/C/D individually (LoRA+ + dropout=0 + alpha=4, stacked on the winning LoRA+ base; arm A/lr=1e-4 deliberately dropped from the stack — it would have pushed LoRA+'s B-matrix LR to ~1.6e-3, close to the 3.2e-3 that diverged in Gemma's own matrix, finding #107). **Combo result: 100% core23 / 76% broad58 / 80% v5-131 — ties plain LoRA+ on core23/broad58 but DROPS v5-131 by 8 pts (88→80).** Stacking dropout=0 + alpha=4 on top of an already-tuned LoRA+ recipe does not help further and costs generalization on the in-distribution set — **plain LoRA+ (arm E) remains the best Qwen3.5-4B recipe.** The combo run needed 3 launch attempts — 2 died to local-network `ConnectionError`/`StreamTerminatedError` mid-training (steps 293 and 613 of 1500) even with `modal run --detach`, confirming this is a recurring infra flakiness class, not a one-off (matches the header's long-standing "flaky local network cancels attached runs" gotcha — `--detach` did not fully protect against it this time). Remaining ~7-pt broad58 gap to Gemma is now more likely architectural (PLE vs DeltaNet) than a tuning gap — no further HP arms planned for Qwen3.5-4B. Runs: `spoke-qwen35-4b-hp-arm-e-loraplus` (seed42, the winner), `spoke-qwen35-4b-hp-combo-loraplus-dropout0-a4-v2` (seed42, the combo), gating runs `spoke-qwen35-4b-hp-gate-seed43/44`.
109. **Merge-script `generation_config.json` fallback silently produces the WRONG eos_token_id when the base model has no upstream generation_config — a RECURRENCE of a 2026-03-06 incident, only ever fixed for one of two export paths.** `merge_adapter_checkpoint.py`, used whenever training runs with `--no-export-merged` (so checkpoints get merged separately per-step), has a fallback: if `/output/{run}/merged/generation_config.json` doesn't exist to copy, it tries `GenerationConfig.from_pretrained(model_name)`. For `Qwen/Qwen3.5-4B` this call raises (the base model ships no `generation_config.json` on the hub at all, confirmed 404) — the `except` block just printed a warning and wrote NOTHING. When `benchmark.py` then loads the merged model, transformers derives a default generation config from the merged `config.json`'s `eos_token_id` field, which for this model is **248044 (`<|endoftext|>`, the base-LM/pad token) — not 248046 (`<|im_end|>`), the actual chat turn-end token the fine-tune emits.** Symptom: every example produces the exact correct answer, then the model runs away for the rest of the 256-token budget hallucinating fake `user`/`assistant` turns (visible as `!` filler — token id 0 in this vocab), scored "partial" instead of "exact" — a 96%→0% collapse that looks like a training catastrophe but is purely a stop-token bug (correct weights, wrong stopping condition; ruled out via clean re-run of the frozen 2026-03-06 champion artifact through the current pipeline, which reproduced 96% cleanly). **This exact failure mode was already hit and "fixed" on 2026-03-06** (`agent-logs/2026-03-06_0403_...md`, commit `ac5b340`) — but that fix only patched the happy path (copying `train_hf.py`'s own export, which apparently gets the eos list right some other way, never identified — possibly a hand-patch on the volume that was never traced to a code fix). The standalone merge script's fallback branch was never actually exercised again until this session, so the underlying bug survived untouched. **Fix (3 files, all unioning the tokenizer's own `eos_token_id` into whatever gets saved rather than trusting `config.json`/`GenerationConfig.from_pretrained` alone): `merge_adapter_checkpoint.py` (the broken fallback), `train_hf.py` (closes the same loose end in the "working" export path, in case it was only ever hand-patched), `benchmark.py` (defense-in-depth — unions at load time too, protecting old volume artifacts and any future producer bug). Also added a runaway-generation alarm to `benchmark.py`** (warns if >20% of examples hit the token budget without stopping naturally) so a broken generation_config reads as one loud log line instead of a burned benchmark cycle next time. Verified: re-merged + re-benchmarked seed-43 post-fix, eos_token_id correctly shows `[248044, 248046]`, latency dropped from ~6.6s/ex back to ~0.46s/ex (matching the March champion), accuracy 96% core23 (matches). **Audit flag: any run merged via `--no-export-merged` against a base model lacking a hub `generation_config.json`, before this fix, has this defect on the volume — don't trust old benchmark numbers from that combination without re-checking.**

The 2 new regressions (#2 spell, #14 emphasis) were in categories that received 0 new examples — collateral damage from reduced per-example repetitions in minority categories (emphasis = 3.6% of data). 25. **Decoder-only is the wrong architecture for editing tasks.** (Research finding, 2025-2026 ASR post-processing literature.) ASR cleanup is fundamentally seq2seq rewriting, not autoregressive generation. Encoder-decoder models (T5, T5Gemma 2) separate input comprehension (encoder) from output generation (decoder with cross-attention), making "preserve A, replace B" natural. This likely explains the persistent compound self-correction failure (#6) — decoder-only Qwen3 processes input and generates output in the same left-to-right pass, making it hard to selectively preserve earlier tokens while replacing later ones. Encoder-decoder evaluation queued as Phase C.
26. **Mixed-bit quantization may close the 9% quant gap.** mlx_lm supports `--quant-predicate mixed_4_6` which allocates 6-bit to critical layers (v_proj, down_proj, lm_head, first/last 12.5% of layers) and 4-bit elsewhere. Critical attention layers are where structural understanding lives — exactly where our quant-sensitive failures (quote-endquote, code-aware) originate. 103. **Apple FM adapter deployment mechanics fully mapped — the install story is the whole reason to pursue this rail (2026-07-06, Apple docs).** User installs ONLY the ~160 MB `.fmadapter`, never the base model — the ~3B foundation model is OS-resident on every Apple Intelligence device, adapter just re-specializes frozen weights (LoRA). Delivery = Background Assets on-demand download (same system-managed path as the native speech models): host `.fmadapter` on a server, declare an asset-downloader extension, OS fetches only the adapter matching that device's exact base-model version. vs shipping our fine-tuned Qwen3-4B = 2.1 GB download → adapter is 13x smaller and rides weights Apple already keeps resident. Runtime: `let a = try SystemLanguageModel.Adapter(name:"spoke"); let m = SystemLanguageModel(adapter:a); let s = LanguageModelSession(model:m)`. **Hard constraint: each adapter is pinned to ONE base-model version — OS update that bumps the model = runtime error until a recompiled adapter downloads (`removeObsoleteAdapters()` cleans old ones). This is the retrain-per-OS-cycle tax the user already accepted.** Training toolkit (v26.0.0, final for OS 26): LoRA PEFT, JSONL chat format (≈ our v5-split, one field rename), needs Mac 32GB+ OR Linux GPU (→ Modal), download bundles base+draft weights (training-only license), Apple example recipe `epochs 5 / lr 1e-3 / batch 4` (100x our Qwen lr — Apple's adapters want aggressive tuning), optional draft-model train → speculative decoding, `export_fmadapter` → ~160 MB package. See finding #98 for zero-shot numbers + guardrail-refusal risk.
102. **Gemma 4 routing is a shared editing-circuit + per-command specialists; emoji is strongly isolated — prune set must be a per-category UNION, not global top-N (2026-07-06, `ifp/profile_by_category.py` + `analyze_by_category.py`).** Decode-mass routing on broad58 (v2), 8 populated categories: 7 text-edit categories (passthrough, spell-replace, multi, self-correction, quote-unquote, disfluency, at-symbol) route through near-identical experts (pairwise cosine 0.88-0.95) — they share one "copy-with-surgical-edit" circuit. **Emoji is the outlier: 0.60-0.78 cosine to everything else, with signature experts at +3.0 mass lift vs +0.7-1.7 for other categories** (emoji fires a dedicated circuit — L26.E113/L0.E81/L27.E25 — because decoding 😀 pulls from an alien vocab region). Milder-but-real signatures: spell-replace (L20.E115), at-symbol (L2.E97). Within-category example pairs route more similarly than across (0.812 vs 0.751, +0.061) — real but modest signal, dominated by the shared circuit. **Consequence for IFP-lite step 2: global top-N-by-mass pruning would silently DELETE emoji (rare → low global mass → pruned, but load-bearing with no fallback). Correct prune set = union of per-category hot experts** (shared circuit + each rare category's specialists). This is also the exact measurement to re-run post-LoRA (finding TODO): does fine-tuning sharpen these signatures (circuit consolidation) or blur them (redistribution)? Caveat: measured on v2 where Gemma scores 45%, so some outputs are wrong; signature strength (esp. emoji) is robust to that. `bycat_*.json` stores per-example decode-mass matrices for offline re-slicing.
101. **Qwen family task-affinity does NOT survive the jump to MoE — Qwen3-30B-A3B loses to Gemma 4 on every axis that matters (B42-B48, 2026-07-06).** Same 2507 generation as the 4B champion, 30B total / 3B active, but zero-shot it scores 43% core23 (clean) and 55% broad58 (spoke-full) — vs Gemma 4's 74%/66%. It is ALSO heavier (20.1 GB wired / 17.3 GB GPU peak, right at the M4's ~18 GB ceiling — triggers the "close to maximum" warning and a 29s cold start) and its dense-4B sibling Qwen3.5-4B actually beat it on lean-prompt broad (41% B26 vs 29% B47). Two behavioral notes: (a) **prompt-insensitive** — generic=v2=v3=43% on core23 where Gemma swung 61-74%, i.e. Qwen applies a fixed policy and ignores rule richness; (b) only few-shot examples move it (spoke-full +26 over v2 on broad), the opposite of the 4B champion's "train lean deploy lean" character (finding #91). **The 35B-A3B sibling would not run at all** — ~19.5 GB weights exceed the Metal GPU working-set limit on 24 GB (OOM/watchdog-kill both times). Takeaway: for this task Gemma 4 A4B is the better MoE on accuracy, RAM, and cold-start; Qwen's only win is warm decode throughput (50 vs 35 tok/s), the least important axis for short dictation outputs. **Cross-family routing result (ifp-lite): the diffuse-union/peaked-mass pattern REPLICATES** — Qwen fires 94.6% of its 6,144 (layer,expert) pairs over broad58 (Gemma 96.3%) yet carries 90% of routing mass on a median 52/128 experts per layer (Gemma 56/128); 41% vs 44% of experts as a fraction. Qwen's decode is slightly MORE diffuse per-pick (top-8 carry 34% vs Gemma's 43% mass) — expected, finer-grained experts over more layers (48 vs 30). Confirms mass-weighted pruning (not fired/not-fired) is the right IFP-lite step-2 approach for BOTH families.
100. **Gemma 4 26B A4B zero-shot SHATTERS the record: 74% core23 (generic) / 66% broad58 (spoke-full) — 8 pts below the fine-tuned champion with ZERO training (B35-B41, 2026-07-06).** Previous zero-shot bests: 35% core23 (Qwen3-4B), 41% broad (Qwen3.5-4B); Apple FM managed 22%/21%. Sparse 26B (4B active) is the first model where scale actually shows up on this task — and it inverts two small-model findings: (a) richer prompts HELP it on broad (spoke-full 66% vs v2 45%; finding #91's "train lean deploy lean" was about ≤4B models — a 26B can actually execute rules from context), (b) prompt sensitivity is large (43-74% on core23). Zero/one hard fails on the good prompts — it behaves, it doesn't refuse (unlike Apple FM), it just misses precision on the same categories as everyone: quote-unquote 0/4 broad (finding #92's big-model quote resistance), multi 1/7. Latencies: 0.97s (generic core23), 3.1s (v2 broad), 9.5s (spoke-full broad, ~1k-token prompt re-prefilled every example — `--prompt-cache` or a fused mid-size prompt would cut this hard). **Implication: LoRA on Gemma 4 A4B is now the most promising accuracy lever besides v6 data** — if fine-tuning adds its usual +30-50 pts over zero-shot, this could be the first model to break 85% broad58. Open questions: does LoRA even fit on M4 (26B base → cloud HF+PEFT; PEFT must target expert weights or just attention?), and does tuned Gemma 4 keep its zero-shot robustness. RAM (17.6 GB wired) is at the edge for a shipped app but fine for a "pro tier on 32GB+ Macs" story.
99. **Gemma 4 26B A4B runs great on the M4 at 4-bit, but Spoke routing is DIFFUSE at the union level — naive expert pruning is dead; mass-weighted pruning stays alive (ifp-lite step 1, 2026-07-06).** Runtime (qat-4bit, `mlx-community/gemma-4-26B-A4B-it-qat-4bit`, 15.6 GB): loads lazily in ~6-10s; **17.6 GB wired total (~14.6 GB model) on 24 GB — fits with room**; warm prefill 178 tok/s, decode 33-36 tok/s; **~1.2-1.5s wall per Spoke example — champion-class latency from a 26B model.** Thinking model: must pass `enable_thinking=False` to the chat template or it burns the whole token budget on a `<|channel>thought` block. Zero-shot behavior looks strong anecdotally (executed at-symbol and exclamation commands cleanly). Routing profile (`ifp/profile_experts.py`, broad58 + v2 prompt, 30 layers x 128 experts, top-8): **a SINGLE example already fires 76% of all 3,840 (layer,expert) pairs; 58 examples fire 96.3%** — the "cold experts never page in" hope is dead, whole model ends up resident, and never-fired pruning saves ~4%. **BUT routing MASS is heavily peaked: median layer carries 50% of assignments on ~14/128 experts, 90% on ~56/128; top-32 experts carry 79% of decode mass, top-64 carry 95.5%.** Decode is more concentrated than prefill (middle layers L10-L16 most concentrated: 90% decode mass on 28-38 experts). Union curve saturates slowly (2926→3698 over 58 examples) — the tail is long but thin. Verdict: IFP-lite step 2 must be **mass-weighted pruning with renormalization** (keep top-N per layer by routed mass, e.g. N=64 halves expert memory ~6 GB), validated by output-vs-unpruned comparison, NOT fired/not-fired pruning. Consistent with the IFP paper's core claim that quality needs *trained* masks — raw routing statistics alone leave 5-10% of assignments orphaned.
